Evolution of Computational Chemistry

Key milestones that transformed chemistry from purely experimental to computationally driven science.

1950s
Birth of Quantum Chemistry
Development of Hartree-Fock methods and the first electronic structure calculations on primitive computers, laying the foundation for computational chemistry.
1970s
Molecular Mechanics Emerges
Introduction of force fields enabling simulation of large biomolecules and polymers, making protein and DNA structure calculations feasible.
1990s
DFT Revolution
Density functional theory becomes practical for routine calculations, earning Kohn and Pople the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1998.
2000s
High-Throughput Computing
Parallel computing and software advances enable virtual screening of millions of compounds, accelerating drug discovery and materials design.
2020s
AI Transforms Chemistry
Machine learning models like AlphaFold and neural network potentials achieve breakthrough accuracy, predicting protein structures and molecular properties at unprecedented speed.
