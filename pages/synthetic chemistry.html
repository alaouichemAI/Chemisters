Evolution of Synthetic Chemistry

Milestone achievements that transformed molecular construction capabilities.

1965
Woodward-Hoffmann Rules
Revolutionary orbital symmetry principles explain pericyclic reactions, earning the Nobel Prize and enabling rational design of complex transformations.
1990
Sharpless Asymmetric Epoxidation
Catalytic enantioselective synthesis becomes routine, revolutionizing pharmaceutical production and earning Sharpless his first Nobel Prize.
2000
Cross-Coupling Revolution
Palladium-catalyzed cross-coupling reactions become ubiquitous in synthesis, later earning Heck, Negishi, and Suzuki the Nobel Prize in 2010.
2010
Photoredox Catalysis Era
Visible light photocatalysis emerges as a powerful tool for organic synthesis, enabling previously inaccessible transformations under mild conditions.
2020s
AI-Driven Synthesis Design
Machine learning algorithms predict synthetic routes, optimize reactions, and autonomously design molecules, transforming how chemists approach synthesis.
