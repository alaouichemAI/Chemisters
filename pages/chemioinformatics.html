Evolution of Chemoinformatics

Key milestones that shaped computational chemistry and data-driven molecular design.

1960s
Birth of SMILES Notation
Development of chemical line notations enables computer representation of molecular structures, laying the foundation for computational chemistry.
1980s
QSAR Revolution
Quantitative structure-activity relationship models emerge, enabling prediction of biological activity from molecular structure descriptors.
2000s
Public Chemical Databases
Launch of PubChem and ChEMBL provides open access to millions of compounds and bioactivity data, democratizing drug discovery research.
2010s
Machine Learning Integration
Deep learning and neural networks transform property prediction, achieving unprecedented accuracy in ADMET and bioactivity forecasting.
2020s
Generative AI Era
Transformer models and graph neural networks enable de novo molecular design, generating novel compounds with desired properties automatically.
