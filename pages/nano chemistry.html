Evolution of Nanochemistry

Key milestones that shaped our ability to manipulate matter at the atomic scale.

1959
Feynman's Vision
Richard Feynman delivers his famous lecture "There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom," envisioning the possibility of manipulating individual atoms and molecules.
1981
Scanning Tunneling Microscope
Binnig and Rohrer invent the STM, enabling visualization and manipulation of individual atoms, earning them the Nobel Prize in 1986.
1985
Discovery of Fullerenes
Buckminsterfullerene (C60) discovered by Kroto, Curl, and Smalley, opening the field of carbon nanomaterials and earning the 1996 Nobel Prize.
1991
Carbon Nanotubes
Iijima reports multi-walled carbon nanotubes, revolutionizing materials science with their extraordinary mechanical and electronic properties.
2010s
Nanomedicine Revolution
FDA approvals of multiple nanoparticle-based drugs and the development of lipid nanoparticles for mRNA vaccine delivery platforms.
2020s
AI-Driven Nanomaterials
Machine learning accelerates discovery and design of nanomaterials with predictive modeling of properties and automated synthesis optimization.
