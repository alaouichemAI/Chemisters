Evolution of Analytical Chemistry

Key milestones that shaped modern analytical science.

1940s
Birth of Modern Chromatography
Martin and Synge develop partition chromatography, revolutionizing separation science and earning the Nobel Prize in 1952.
1960s
NMR Enters Chemistry
Nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy becomes commercially available, transforming structural elucidation in organic chemistry.
1980s
Mass Spec Revolution
Development of electrospray ionization and MALDI enables analysis of large biomolecules, earning Nobel Prizes for Fenn and Tanaka.
2000s
Miniaturization Era
Lab-on-chip and microfluidic technologies emerge, enabling portable analysis and point-of-care diagnostics.
2020s
AI Integration
Machine learning transforms data analysis, spectrum prediction, and automated method development in analytical chemistry.
